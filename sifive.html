SiFive Cloud Services

Product: Desktop web apps

Role: UX research & design, visual design

Background

When I began work at SiFive as their first product designer hire, I was put to work on their planned SaaS products for the semiconductor industry: SiFive Core Designer, for configuring CPU cores (the part of a microchip that does general computing tasks), and SiFive Chip Designer, for configuring and designing finished microchips.

Core Designer and Chip Designer presented many design challenges. Both apps, which were to support highly specialized and technical workflows, establish new product categories without direct competitors, so there were no established UI patterns from comparable products to refer to.

Core Designer has launched and used internally at SiFive and by customers such as Intel and Qualcomm. Not only a product that is sold in its own right, Core Designer is employed in our sales process, allowing prospective customers to quickly spec out and generate CPU cores for evaluation.

Initial Design and Development

After extensive discussions with CPU design engineers as well as other stakeholders and prospective users, I redesigned the Core Designer UI with the help of many people. Because it is a farily unique product, I had to solve some interesting problems due to constraints relating to CPU design and our technology in particular.

Before the launch of Core Designer, SiFive had engaged with a design firm to create SiFive's new brand style and website. The visual design of Core Designer was refreshed to reflect the new brand style and the website's look and conventions. For example, the "start page" shown here uses signature graphics that the design firm had created to give each of our CPU cores a unique graphic identity.

Core Designer Refreshed UI

We probably followed the styling of the website a bit too closely in Core Designer, because the current styling has some drawbacks, such as poor contrast, low information density, overuse of bright brand colors in the block diagram, and other issues. I've included a mockup of a refreshed style that attempts to retain the essence of the brand style while correcting some of the aforementioned problems.

SiFive Chip Designer

Chip Designer is an ambitious product that will enable users to create customized microchips through a cloud-based application.

I designed the main UI to follow the pattern of apps such as XCode and Keynote, with a main canvas in the center, and a properties panel on the right. The properties area normally shows general info about the chip, but when a particular IP block on the chip is selected, the properties area shows settings and properties for that IP.

The left panel shows a library of IP blocks that are available to include on the chip. Clicking an item reveals a popover that shows more information.

The "Area Inspector" shown is a concept for a little utility, shown in a modal pop-up, to give a detailed view of how various IP blocks are taking up space on a chip. Managing the area budget is one of the more important aspects of SoC (system-on-chip) design. I took inspiration from utilities for managing hard drive space.
