Experience

November 2015 – April 2017

Product Designer

Seneca Systems

At Seneca Systems, a company backed by Y Combinator, Initialized Capital, and Cowboy Ventures, I was sole designer for Romulus, a CRM for local governments; conducting user research and creating wireframes, task flows, and visual design. Romulus has been adopted by city governments nationwide, including Chicago, Houston, Minneapolis, Oakland, Miami, and Boston.

I collaborated with our CEO, CTO, salespersons, and customer-experience staff to set the product's direction. Working closely with engineers in an Agile environment, I managed our project backlog and led planning meetings and sprint retrospectives.

I wrote UI copy and help documentation as well as promotional copy for our public website.

November 2013 – April 2015

UX Designer/Developer

i360 LLC

At i360, a data analysis company for political analysis, I served as a UX designer, visual designer, and front-end dev in an Agile environment.

I designed iOS and Android user interfaces for two mobile applications: i360 Walk, which supported door-to-door canvassers, and i360 Calls, which was part of a phone-canvassing system. Both apps were employed by thousands of volunteers and garnered praise for from i360’s clients for their ease of use. I additionally designed and coded HTML/CSS for a web-based, desktop version of i360 Calls.

I also designed and refined features on a Web-based portal that incorporated various complex functions for gathering and analyzing political data. I led an initiative to re-write the HTML/CSS for this web application based on the Object-Oriented CSS approach.

May 2011 – September 2013

Art Director & Senior Editor

American Ideas Institute / TAC

At TAC, in addition to editorial tasks, I was responsible for the design and art direction of the print magazine and website.

I also adapted TAC to the Kindle and produced a Kindle edition for each issue, and designed and produced the template for a weekly e-mail newsletter.

I led a redesign of TAC’s website, creating wireframes and design comps, producing HTML/CSS, and working with developers to integrate the design with a Wordpress-based CMS. Since the redesign, traffic increased threefold, with one month bring more than two million page views.
