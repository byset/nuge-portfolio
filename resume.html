Experience

Feb. 2018 – Present, San Mateo, Calif.

Senior Staff Designer, SiFive

At SiFive, I am UX designer for our SaaS products, primarily Core Designer, web-based software for configuring microprocessor cores, and Chip Designer, a cloud service for assembling components of silicon chips.

I work closely with engineers, product managers, executives, and salespeople to design products that solve our users’ problems while supporting our business objectives. My activities at SiFive include user research, UI design, prototyping, visual design, and helping to manage our feature roadmap.

Core Designer is now used by customers at Qualcomm, Intel, and other well-known companies.

Nov. 2015 – Apr. 2017, Redwood City, Calif.

Product Designer, Seneca Systems

At Seneca Systems, a company backed by Y Combinator, Initialized Capital, and Cowboy Ventures, I was sole designer for Romulus, a CRM for local governments; conducting user research and creating wireframes, task flows, and visual design. Romulus was adopted by city governments nationwide, including Chicago, Houston, Minneapolis, Oakland, Miami, and Boston.

I collaborated with our CEO, CTO, salespersons, and customer-experience staff to set the product's direction. Working closely with engineers in an Agile environment, I managed our project backlog and led planning meetings and sprint retrospectives.

I wrote UI copy and help documentation as well as promotional copy for our public website.

Nov 2013 – Apr 2015, Arlington, Va.

UX Designer/Developer, i360

At i360, a data analysis company for political analysis, I served as a UX designer, visual designer, and front-end dev in an Agile environment.

I designed iOS and Android user interfaces for two mobile applications: i360 Walk, which supported door-to-door canvassers, and i360 Calls, which was part of a phone-canvassing system. Both apps were employed by thousands of volunteers and garnered praise for from i360’s clients for their ease of use. I additionally designed and coded HTML/CSS for a web-based, desktop version of i360 Calls.

I also designed and refined features on a Web-based portal that incorporated various complex functions for gathering and analyzing political data. I led an initiative to re-write the HTML/CSS for this web application based on the Object-Oriented CSS approach.

May 2011 – September 2013

Art Director & Senior Editor, American Ideas Institute

At TAC Magazine, in addition to editorial tasks, I was responsible for design and art direction for the website and print edition.

I also adapted TAC to the Kindle and produced a Kindle edition for each issue, and designed and produced the template for a weekly e-mail newsletter.

I led a redesign of TAC’s website, creating wireframes and design comps, producing HTML/CSS, and working with developers to integrate the design with a Wordpress-based CMS. Since the redesign, traffic increased threefold, with one month bring more than two million page views.

July 2009 – May 2011

Associate Editor, The American Interest

My art direction duties for The American Interest included magazine layout, designing spreads, creating illustration concepts, and commissioning illustrations from artists.

I also created wireframes and design comps for a redesign of the AI's website. From this design I produced Wordpress templates for the AI's blogs, including Walter Russell Mead's popular “Via Meadia” blog.

January 2008 – April 2009

Web Designer, Hanley Wood

Hanley Wood is a media and e-comerce company focused on its family of trade publications for the building industry. At Hanley-Wood much of my work consisted of producing HTML and CSS during a transition of their online publications to the Tridion CMS. I also did design and coding work on their network of sites that sell house plans online.
