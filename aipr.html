American Interest layout

print design
Click on an image to enlarge.

As art director of the American Interest's interior pages (a colleague was responsible for designing covers), I redesigned its standard article templates using InDesign. I also commissioned illustrations and cartoons for use in our pages. I myself created many charts, maps, and other graphics for the magazine. Shown here are a few of the many print layouts I created for the AI.

To open a symposium on the future of Europe, I created an illustration of the stars of the EU flag disintegrating into a question mark.

For a Robert Reich piece on the rise of populism in America, I doctored Delacroix's Liberty Leading the People by changing the flag and adding a piece of Manhattan in the background.

An article on state fairs offered the chance to have some fun with type.
