i360 Calls for iOS and Android

My employer, i360, needed a mobile front end to its Predictive Dialer application. The Predictive Dialer lets customers — typically political campaigns or advocacy groups — administer phone surveys to targeted lists of voters.

I was responsible for all phases of design for this app, from wireframes to design comps for iOS and Android through testing for design issues, as well as writing copy for prompts, labels, and error messages.

i360 Calls for Web

Our rewrite of the browser-based version of i360 Calls allowed me to take a redesign from the conceptual and mockup stage through writing HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. By designing and participating in the development of this application, I was able to make improvements to its aesthetics, usability, and front-end code.

Inside China's Kleptocracy

The Kleptocracy Initiative, a Hudson Institute project, asked me to create an interactive inforgraphic to help illustrate the personal and business networks top Chinese political leaders use to amass and hide their fortunes.

I created the graphic using Sketch, HTML, LESS/CSS, and CSS3 animations. The Hudson Institute was pleased with the result and I am currently working on a similar project for them.

Czardinals logo

I produced this logo/t-shirt design for the Office of National Drug Control Policy's softball team, the Czardinals (named after the Drug Czar). To do so, I created a sort of mashup of the seal of the Executive Office of the President, of which ONDCP is a part, with a cartoon cardinal from a late-50s scorecard.

Francis Fukuyama needed maps for his upcoming book The Origins of Political Order. I produced nine maps in Adobe Illustrator. Dr. Fukuyama was happy with the results, and would ask me to help him formats charts to the second (and final) book of the series.
